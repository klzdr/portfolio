Information Assurance

Making sure information is safe and reliable. This includes protecting data from being stolen, changed, or lost.

  • Integrity: Info stays correct and trustworthy
  • Availability: Info is ready when needed
  • Confidentiality: Only authorized people can see it

The CIA Triad (Core Security Principles)

Confidentiality

Info should only be seen by the right people.

  • Examples: messages, personal data, healthcare records, intellectual property
  • How to protect it: Passwords, Two-factor authentication (2FA), Data encryption
Integrity

Info should stay accurate and reliable.

  • Examples: bank transactions, edited documents, online reviews, databases
  • How to protect it: Data validation, Version control systems, Hashing and checksums
Availability

Info should always be accessible to authorized users, even if systems fail.

  • Examples: healthcare systems, online shopping, cloud services
  • How to protect it: DDoS protection, Backups, Load balancers
